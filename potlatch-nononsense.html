About Pot-latch
The potlatch takes many forms across the Pacific Northwest. Pot-latch, the platform, draws on a tradition among the Kwakwaka'wakw people, where a child's milestone was marked by the whole community gathering around them, giving together, witnessing the moment.
That spirit of collective giving is what we're building on.
How it works
1
Create your invitation. Add your party details — date, time, location. No mention of gifts.
2
Set up the gift. Choose what your child wishes for, set a maximum contribution, and let Pot-latch handle the rest.
3
Share on WhatsApp. Copy the auto-generated message and paste it into your parent group.
4
Guests choose after they RSVP. They can simply come and celebrate or contribute to the gift's idea. Both options are always equally visible.
5
Ask a friend to take the lead. Pass the gift share message to someone you trust — they send it to the group, like a proper gift champion!
Past ideas
🌱 A friendship garden
Each guest brings a small plant or seed packet. Together, everyone plants a friendship garden — a living reminder of the people who came to celebrate.
📚 A book swap
Guests bring a favourite book and take one home. Stories shared, stories discovered.
🍎 Food for a food bank
Each guest brings a tin or packet of food. After the party, everyone delivers it to the local food bank together.
🔧 Toy repair workshop
Guests bring a broken toy. A repair expert helps children fix and upcycle them.
FAQ
How do I set a contribution limit?
When setting up the gift, you choose a maximum amount per person. Guests can contribute anything up to that amount — or nothing at all.
Will guests know what others contributed?
No. Contributions are anonymised by default. Neither the host nor other guests can see individual amounts.
What if more money is raised than needed?
Any surplus goes to the charity of your child's choice. Nothing is wasted.
What if a guest does not want to contribute?
They simply choose "just come and celebrate" — always equally visible, never buried.
